# Milestone 1 — Proof of Concept

## Goal
The goal of this stage was to determine whether a basic system could transfer vibration from a speaker to a steel plate in a way that produces any visible movement in salt.

At this point, the focus was not on pattern quality, but simply on whether the system could respond at all.

---

## Setup
- Thrifted speaker used as sound source  
- Audio amplifier connected to speaker  
- Steel plate mounted above speaker  
- Salt placed on surface  

A very early version of the mechanical coupling system was used, with minimal optimization.

---

## Observations
- Vibration transfer was weak and inconsistent  
- Salt movement was minimal or uneven  
- No stable nodal patterns formed  
- System response varied depending on frequency  

---

## Key Insight
At this stage, it became clear that simply generating sound was not enough.

The system required:
- Better structural stability  
- More efficient energy transfer  
- A deeper understanding of resonance conditions  

This introduced the idea that **physical structure determines whether sound becomes visible behavior**.

---

## Outcome
This milestone confirmed that the concept was viable, but not yet functional in a meaningful way.
