# Milestone 3 — Refinement & System Limits

## Goal
The goal of this stage was to refine the system for more reliable pattern formation and explore whether more complex nodal structures could be achieved.

---

## Final System Improvements
- Transitioned from breadboard amplifier to soldered circuit  
- Replaced cardboard enclosure with a rigid thrifted cigar box  
- Stabilized mechanical coupling system  
- Improved overall structural rigidity  

---

## Observations
- Simple patterns became reproducible  
- System responded more consistently at certain frequencies  
- Complex patterns were difficult to sustain  
- Small physical changes significantly affected results  

---

## Key Limitation
Despite improvements, the system showed clear constraints:

- Speaker power limited vibration intensity  
- Steel plate properties restricted possible resonance modes  
- Lack of precision frequency control reduced repeatability of complex patterns  

---

## Key Insight: Physical Constraints Matter
This stage reinforced that resonance is not just a frequency property—it is a **system property**.

Even when the correct frequency is used, patterns may fail if the physical system does not support the required energy distribution.

---

## Outcome
The system successfully demonstrated resonance-based pattern formation, but also revealed the gap between theoretical models and real-world implementation.
